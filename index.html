Biodynamic Craniosacral Therapy

How does it work?

Throughout life, our bodies adapt to physical injuries, emotional stress, illness, and the demands of everyday life. Sometimes these experiences leave patterns of tension that can affect how we feel, move, think, and relate to others.

The name craniosacral refers to the cranium (the skull) and the sacrum (the triangular bone at the base of the spine). Together, they form the boundaries of the craniosacral system, which houses and protects the brain, spinal cord, and cerebrospinal fluid.

Biodynamic Craniosacral Therapy is based on the understanding that the body has an inherent capacity to heal and reorganize itself. During our session, I gently listen to the subtle rhythms expressed throughout your body, including the movement of the cerebrospinal fluid that surrounds and protects the brain and spinal cord.

Using gentle, non-invasive touch and attentive presence, I support your nervous system in settling and reconnecting with its natural resources. As your body shifts into a state of greater ease, held tension may begin to release, allowing balance, resilience, and wellbeing to emerge naturally.

Every session is unique because it follows what your body needs in that moment.
